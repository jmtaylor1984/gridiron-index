A position learning library families can use all season.

Gridiron Index is a curated index of open-access football instruction — organized by side of the ball, position, and source-tier. It installs to a phone or tablet home screen, opens offline after the first load, and helps a player or parent find a credible teaching video in two taps. Videos themselves are linked, not hosted; the product is the curation, the organization, and the always-available app shell.

Installable

Adds to iOS, Android, and desktop home screens. Standalone window, app icon, no browser chrome.

Offline-ready

App shell and the entire archive cache on first load. The library opens with no signal at a field, gym, or practice facility.

Source-tier filtering

Every entry is tagged Elite, Trusted, or Curated based on speaker credentials and source reputability.

Position-first navigation

Browse offense or defense, drill into a position group, search by skill, coach, drill, or scheme keyword.

About the archive

Method & quality controls

How videos were sourced, why poor-review filtering required an approximation, and what each badge means.

Sourcing & selection

Entries were gathered by searching YouTube for open-access football coaching instruction tied to a specific position. Searches prioritized recognized national coaching organizations (AFCA, Glazier Clinics), established coaching educators (MatchQuarters / Cody Alexander, All Eyes DB Camp / Chad Wilson), USA Football, and named college or NFL coaches. Speaker credentials were confirmed from the video title, description, or on-screen title cards wherever possible.

Why poor-review videos are not flagged by rating

YouTube removed public dislike counts in November 2021, and aggregate user-review data for individual videos is not reliably available to outside tools. As a result, no poor-review videos were intentionally included, but the archive does not filter by dislike or review score — that data simply isn't accessible in a trustworthy form. Quality filtering was instead approximated by four working criteria:

  • Reputable source — national coaching associations, established clinics, or recognized educator channels.
  • Named, credentialed speaker — identified in the title, description, or on-screen, with a verifiable coaching role.
  • Direct position relevance — instructional content tied to a specific position group, not general football commentary.
  • Coaching value — drill specificity, technique breakdown, or scheme-applicable detail useful to a position coach.

TikTok, Instagram, and Facebook clips were excluded by design. Videos from unclear or anonymous sources were excluded unless uniquely valuable for a position not otherwise well covered.

Tier definitions

Elite

National coaching association (AFCA, Glazier), established coaching educator, or NFL / Power-conference head coach with verifiable credentials.

Trusted

Named FBS, college, or pro coach. Clinic, convention, or position-room presentation with verifiable speaker.

Curated

Position-specific instructional content selected for direct coaching value. Speaker identified where possible.

Notes & limitations

Durations are drawn from search metadata where available and are approximate. URLs link to publicly available YouTube videos; access status is the public access status at the time of curation. This archive contains references and links only — no video content is hosted here.
